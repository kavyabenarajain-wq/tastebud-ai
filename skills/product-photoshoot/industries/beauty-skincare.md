# Beauty & Skincare — Product Photoshoot Playbook

Loaded by [product-photoshoot/SKILL.md](../SKILL.md) when the uploaded product is skincare. The engine owns the realism bar, brand-lock, six-angle system, and QC. This file owns the **taste**: how skincare specifically has to be shot to read like the category's best work.

> Makeup and fragrance are separate playbooks. Colour cosmetics → [industries/makeup.md](makeup.md); perfume/cologne/mist → [industries/fragrance.md](fragrance.md). The three Beauty sub-types look nothing alike: skincare is neutral, calm, clinical-but-warm; makeup is chromatic, indulgent, high-energy; fragrance is moody, cinematic, sensual.

---

## What skincare is really selling

Skincare sells a **result you can't see yet** — clarity, hydration, calm, glow. So the imagery has to feel **clean, dewy, trustworthy, and quietly premium**. The two things this category must nail:

1. **Substance truth** — the amber translucency of an oil, the matte peak of a cream, the slick of a gel, condensation on glass, a droplet at a dropper tip. This is where the realism is won.
2. **Calm, considered space** — lots of negative space, deliberate staging, a spa-quiet mood. Crowded or loud reads cheap here.

---

## The shot archetypes

Pick the archetype per shot. A strong skincare set mixes 3–4; it never returns six packshots.

### 1. Hand-held hero — *the dominant skincare move*
Real hands holding the bottle/jar, often up near the face; frequently several hands fanned out holding the range. Bare, slightly dewy skin, neutral nails.
- **Recipe:** soft directional light, neutral backdrop, real skin texture; product turned so the label stays legible; a droplet or sheen if relevant.
- **Why:** shows scale, signals "this touches your body", humanises a sterile bottle.

### 2. Sunlit still-life with hard shadows — *the category signature*
Product on sand/oat/linen, raked by hard directional sun throwing crisp palm-frond or window-blind shadows. Warm, golden, "morning light through a window."
- **Recipe:** warm hard key from one side; neutral natural surface (linen, sand, plaster); a foliage or blind gobo for the shadow; generous negative space.
- **Why:** instantly premium, natural, editorial.

### 3. Plinth / stone podium minimal
Single product on travertine, a raw rock, or a cream paper sweep with a soft gradient. Spa-calm luxury, heavy negative space.
- **Recipe:** soft even-to-directional light, monochrome neutral set, product centred or rule-of-thirds with air around it.
- **Why:** signals luxury and confidence.

### 4. Water & splash
Product half-submerged with ripples, condensation beads, or in a pool. Communicates hydration/freshness literally.
- **Recipe:** clean water with believable refraction and surface tension; real droplets and caustics; cool or neutral tone.
- **Why:** makes "hydration" visible.

### 5. Ingredient pairing — *the active, shown*
Product shot **with its hero ingredient**: peach squeezed over the bottle, honey drip, aloe leaf, citrus or oranges in a net bag, fresh tomato. (Skincare pairs the *active*; makeup pairs *craving* — keep that distinction.)
- **Recipe:** fresh, just-placed produce; the ingredient interacts with the product (a drip, a slice beside it); colour echoes the formula.
- **Why:** tells the "what's inside" story in one frame.

### 6. Texture / swatch macro
Cream smear, oil drip, gel swatch on the back of a hand, dropper mid-drop.
- **Recipe:** macro lens feel, raking light to carve texture; honour viscosity — cream peaks and holds, oil beads and runs slow, gel reads wet and slick, toner moves like water.
- **Why:** proves the formula; where realism is won.

### 7. Floating / levitation
Products suspended against a gradient or sky. Playful, modern — best for colourful younger brands.
- **Recipe:** clean gradient/sky, consistent soft shadowing so floats still obey one light logic.

### 8. Range lineup
The full set as an editorial group — establishes the line as a system.
- **Recipe:** one light, one surface, staggered depth or a considered flat lay; a clear hero; never a flat police-lineup row unless the brand wants a catalogue look.

## Craft constants

- **Light:** either hard warm directional sun (crisp foliage shadows) *or* soft diffused north-light. Almost never flat/even.
- **Palette:** **neutral dominates** — sand / oat / cream / beige; soft pastels second; saturated gradients only for youthful colour-brands. Backgrounds recede so the product and substance lead. (This is the opposite of makeup, where the backdrop is keyed to the shade.)
- **Surfaces:** linen, travertine/stone, cream paper sweep, acrylic risers, water.
- **Props:** the hero active ingredient, glass/acrylic risers, towels, fresh produce in net bags, foliage — chosen sparingly.
- **Hands + dewy skin** recur because they carry scale, warmth, and use-context.
- **Mood:** clean, dewy, clinical-but-warm, Korean-minimal, spa-quiet. Negative space is a feature, not a gap.

---

## Substance & material specifics (extends the engine's substance rules)

- **Serum / oil:** true amber/clear translucency, believable fill (half unless there's a reason), a droplet hanging at the dropper tip or a slow bead down the glass. Oil moves slow and beads; toner moves like water; thick serum strings and clings.
- **Cream / balm / butter:** a scooped peak, glossy or matte surface, a swatch with real spread and a soft edge. Whipped looks airy, balm dense, gel wet.
- **Glass & frosted bottles:** refraction through glass and liquid, caustics on the surface, frosted diffuses to a soft glow, one controlled shoulder highlight — never a blown-out blob.
- **Freshness/temperature cues:** condensation and small water beads on cool/fresh products; use to sell realism, never to clutter.
- **SPF / lotion / body:** believable dispense (pump drop, lotion swatch), matte-to-dewy finish true to the formula.

The brand's text, logo, and finish stay sharp and legible throughout — warped label text is an instant reject.

## Default move (safe, on-brand skincare recipe)

When the brief is thin: **warm directional light → neutral sand/stone/linen surface → either a held-in-hand hero or a sunlit still-life → pair with the hero ingredient → nail the substance (oil/cream/gel) → keep generous negative space.** Pull exact palette, surface, and mood from the Brand Profile first.

---

## Six-angle adaptation

The engine's six angles still apply; bias them for skincare:
- **Macro close-up** carries the substance (droplet, swatch, condensation) — always include one.
- **Three-quarter** is usually the lead hero for bottles and jars.
- A **hand-held** or **sunlit still-life** frame often beats a plain front packshot as the campaign image.
- Keep light, surface, and treatment consistent so the set reads as one quiet, premium shoot.

---

## Category do-not list (on top of the Brand Profile's)

- No loud, cluttered, or chromatic staging that fights the product — skincare reads premium when calm.
- No flat, shadowless lighting (kills the substance and the mood).
- No empty or inert product — show the formula behaving.
- No plastic/CGI skin on hands — real pores and texture.
- No fake-looking water or impossible refraction.
- No warped label text or invented ingredient claims.
- Don't over-prop — when in doubt, remove a prop and add space.
