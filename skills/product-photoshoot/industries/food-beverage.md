# Food & Beverage — Product Photoshoot Playbook

Loaded by [product-photoshoot/SKILL.md](../SKILL.md) when the uploaded product is food or drink. The engine owns the realism bar, brand-lock, six-angle system, and QC. This file owns the **taste**: how F&B specifically has to be shot.

> One industry, two substance-worlds: **food** (the snack itself — crunch, spill, texture) and **drink** (liquid — fizz, condensation, ice, pour). They share the same loud visual DNA but diverge sharply on substance. First decide which you're shooting, then apply the shared archetypes + the right substance rules.

---

## What F&B is really selling — and why it looks nothing like Beauty

Beauty recedes into neutral sand and quiet light. **F&B screams.** Modern D2C food and drink lives on saturated, flavour-keyed colour, hard sunny shadows, hands, motion, and abundance. The emotional job is:

- **Food → appetite.** Make it look craveable: crunchy, fresh, fall-into-the-pile abundant.
- **Drink → refreshment.** Make it look *ice cold* and thirst-quenching: condensation, ice, fizz, sunlight.

If a F&B shot doesn't trigger crave or thirst, it has failed — however clean the packaging looks.

Two things must both be true in the best shots: **the packaging is the hero** (brand recognition — the can, bag, bottle) **and the contents show** (the chip, the liquid, the fizz — proof of what's inside).

---

## The shot archetypes

Pick the archetype per shot. A strong F&B set mixes 3–4; it never returns six packshots.

### 1. Bold colour-block studio — *the dominant F&B move*
Punchy saturated seamless (yellow, pink, orange, red, blue, green), often a two-tone diagonal split, hard crisp shadows, packaging popping off the colour.
- **Recipe:** background keyed to the **flavour or brand** (orange backdrop for orange flavour, pink for berry, green for lime); one hard directional light with a sharp shadow; product centred and confident.
- **Proves:** brand energy + makes the pack unmissable. This is F&B's signature, the way swatch is makeup's.

### 2. Contents spill / the money shot — *appetite & proof*
Chips, puffs, gummies, cereal cascading out of the bag; the snack piled in abundance. For drinks: the **pour** into a glass with fizz.
- **Recipe (food):** believable spill physics, real crumbs, generous pile; the pack open and tilting.
- **Recipe (drink):** a real liquid stream, bubbles and a crown where it lands, ice.
- **Proves:** what's inside, and abundance = value.

### 3. Hands holding / interacting
Hand gripping the bag or can, multiple hands, a hand reaching in for a chip, two cans clinking in a "cheers," a hand presenting the product.
- **Recipe:** real skin, believable grip; energy and motion welcome; often against a colour-block.
- **Proves:** scale, social energy, "grab one."

### 4. Flavour / ingredient pairing
The product with its flavour source: chips with cheese cubes, apple tea with apple slices + cinnamon sticks, a berry drink with strawberries, a citrus can buried in lemons, a coffee latte beside coffee beans.
- **Recipe:** fresh, glossy, just-placed ingredients that echo the flavour and the pack colour; product stays hero.
- **Proves:** the flavour story in one frame.

### 5. Lifestyle in-context
Picnic, beach, pool, gym, desk, fridge shelf, denim pocket, tote/net bag, skateboard, couch-snacking.
- **Recipe:** sunny natural light, casual real styling, the moment of consumption; props for the brand's persona.
- **Proves:** where and when it's enjoyed.

### 6. Levitation / dynamic toss
Bag flying with contents exploding mid-air, cans tumbling or stacked, product floating.
- **Recipe:** clean colour or sky ground; consistent light/shadow so floats still obey one logic; arrangement reads designed.
- **Proves:** playful, modern, high-energy — strong for youthful brands.

### 7. Range lineup on colour
The full flavour range arranged — on podium risers or a gradient — flavour variety shown as colour variety.
- **Recipe:** one light, one surface; a clear hero or a rhythmic row; each flavour's pack true and legible.
- **Proves:** the line as a system, the flavour choices.

### 8. Texture / crunch (food) or chill macro (drink)
**Food:** macro on the chip, granola, gummy, chocolate snap — appetite and texture. **Drink:** macro on the cold can beaded with condensation, ice, the fizz at the surface.
- **Proves:** the sensory promise — crunch or cold.

## Craft constants

- **Colour is loud and flavour-coded.** Unlike Beauty's neutrals, F&B lives on saturated colour-block — usually keyed to the flavour or brand (like makeup keys to the shade). High energy, playful, often Gen-Z.
- **Hard light, crisp shadows.** Sunny, punchy, high-key directional light with sharp shadows is the default. Drinks especially lean sunlit and summery.
- **Appetite + refreshment are the emotional core.** Food = crunchy/fresh/craveable. Drink = ice cold/thirst-quenching. Everything serves this.
- **Packaging hero + contents proof together.** Sell the pack (recognition) and show what's inside (the chip, the liquid, the fizz).
- **Human energy** — hands, motion, lifestyle. F&B is social and casual, not still and reverent.
- **The heritage/premium exception.** Coffee, chocolate, tea, and "artisan" goods can go moodier and warmer — wood, linen, dark backdrops, rustic still life (a granola jar, cinnamon tea on deep red, a chocolate snap). Not everything is neon; read the brand. Premium F&B borrows from the Beauty playbook's calm. For coffee/matcha/tea specifically, see the dedicated **Prepared & café beverages** mode below.

## Snacks (chips · puffs · gummies · candy · popcorn · crackers · bars · nuts · makhana)

The loudest, most social corner of F&B and the one with the densest visual playbook. Use this mode for any packaged hand-snack. It inherits everything above but sharpens the emphasis: for snacks the **hand holding the bag against colour is the hero move** (not the spill), the mood is **young, playful, hyper-social**, and the bag itself — its crinkle, gloss, and standing form — is half the shot.

**Core insight:** snacks sell a **moment, not a meal** — the grab, the share, the crunch between other things (games, a picnic, the couch, the grocery run). The packaging is almost always *in someone's hand or against a punchy colour*, rarely sitting alone on a plinth. Abundance + energy + flavour-colour.

### Snack archetypes (refine the general F&B set)

1. **Hand-holds-the-bag on colour-block — *the signature snack move.*** A single hand (or two) gripping the standing bag/pouch against a saturated seamless keyed to the flavour; crisp hard shadow; bag crinkle and tension real. The most repeated shot in the whole category — make it the default hero.
2. **The paper / surface tear-through reveal.** A hand punching or reaching *through* a coloured paper backdrop (or through the bag's own torn foil) to pull out a chip — the snack breaking the plane. Graphic, modern, very D2C.
3. **Social eating — *the snack's reason to exist.*** Groups laughing and sharing on a couch/picnic/floor, a model mid-bite, a hand reaching into a shared bowl, friends passing the bag, "you deserve a treat." Real candid energy, sunny or warm light. Snacks are communal — this carries the brand's personality more than any packshot.
4. **Games & activity flat-lay.** The snack staged into a play moment — scrabble, chess, cards, checkerboard, controllers, dominoes — bag open, contents spilling among the props. Overhead or low three-quarter. Sells "snack while you hang."
5. **Held at the body / pocket / bag.** Bag tucked in a denim pocket or waistband, peeking from a tote or backpack, hugged to the chest, carried out of the grocery aisle. The on-the-go, "comes with me" story.
6. **Multipack hug / range fan in hands.** Several flavours fanned across multiple hands or cradled in both arms against colour — flavour variety shown as a colourful armful.
7. **Saturated colour-block studio with risers.** Bag on podiums/acrylic blocks against a punchy seamless, a bowl of the snack beside it, hard sharp shadow — the clean catalogue-but-loud hero. Strong for gummies/candy where the contents are themselves vivid (gummy bears on candy rings, blue stars on blue, sour candy on pink).
8. **Spill / cascade & crunch macro.** Chips, puffs, gummies, popcorn, makhana tumbling from the bag in believable physics; then a macro on the ridge, the sugar crystals, the puff's matte, the chocolate snap. Appetite + texture proof.

### Snack substance & styling rules

- **The bag is a character.** Standing pouches, gussets, resealable zips, foil vs. matte litho — render the crinkle, tension, and shelf-standing form true. A limp or geometrically-wrong bag is an instant tell.
- **Contents look craveable, never stale:** oil sheen and ridge on chips, the matte dust on a puff, sugar crystals and translucency on gummies/candy, the snap and gloss on chocolate-coated, fresh-fried colour on makhana/nuts. Real crumbs in the spill.
- **Hands are everywhere** — real skin, believable grip, mid-grab or mid-bite motion. Snack photography is kinetic; static is the exception.
- **Flavour = colour, loud.** Key the seamless to the flavour/variant (BBQ→red/orange, sour→green/pink, salted→blue/yellow, berry→purple/pink). Candy and gummies can go fully chromatic and bright.
- **Legible flavour name & callouts** (protein, baked, vegan, spice level) stay sharp — warped flavour text is an instant reject.

### Snack default move

When the brief is thin for a snack: **key a saturated colour-block to the flavour → hard light, crisp shadow → lead with a hand gripping the standing bag (or a tear-through reveal) → add a bowl of the contents or a believable spill for proof → reach for a social-eating or games frame for personality.** Keep it young, abundant, and kinetic. Pull exact palette and persona from the Brand Profile first.

## Chocolate & confectionery (bars · bonbons · pralines · truffles · filled · bean-to-bar)

A substance-world of its own — distinct enough from snacks to warrant its own mode. Use it for any chocolate or chocolate-forward confection: moulded bars, enrobed bars, bonbons and pralines, truffles, filled/centred pieces, dragées, the box of chocolates, and bean-to-bar/craft. It inherits the F&B core (appetite, packaging-hero + contents-proof, loud-or-warm read from the brand) but chocolate has its own physics and its own three colour-worlds.

**Core insight:** chocolate sells **indulgence and the moment of giving in** — gloss, melt, the snap, the slow ooze. The contents are the hero far more than the wrapper; a chocolate shot lives or dies on **temper and substance**, not packaging. And the category splits into three brand-worlds — *read the brand and commit to one*:

- **Luxury / gifting** → dark, moody, low-key; burgundy, deep brown, near-black, gold accents; velvet, slate, polished stone; bokeh, a single hero bonbon; the open box, ribbon, Valentine/Christmas. Reverent and still — borrows from the Beauty playbook's calm.
- **Playful / D2C** → saturated colour-block (pink, green, purple, red, blue), hard crisp shadows, hands, games and graphic staging; young, social, kinetic — the loud F&B signature applied to chocolate.
- **Artisan / bean-to-bar** → warm cocoa-brown monochrome; cocoa dust, raw cocoa pods and beans, the mould tray; linen, wood, stone; provenance and craft, rustic and tactile.

### Chocolate archetypes

1. **The molten pour / cascade — *the signature chocolate move.*** Tempered couverture streaming and dripping over a bar, a bonbon, a stack, or a piled mound; thick glossy ribbons, a pool with a slow crown, a single drip caught hanging. Honey-thick, never watery. The most-repeated hero in the category — make it the default.
2. **The snap / break.** A bar fractured mid-frame, broken squares stacked or scattered, the clean crack with sharp edges; the matte fractured interior against the glossy shell. Proves temper and freshness. Hands snapping a bar is the kinetic version.
3. **Cross-section / the oozing centre — *the appetite money shot for filled chocolate.*** The bite or the cut piece with the centre flowing — caramel stringing and pulling, ganache dense and soft, liquid caramel running, the hazelnut/nougat/wafer layers laid bare. If the piece is filled, this shot must exist; it sells what the outside can't.
4. **Bonbon / praline hero on moody dark.** One or a few glossy domed shells (often with coloured cocoa-butter splatter or a sprayed velvet finish) shot low-key with deep shadow and bokeh — luxury, jewel-like, reverent.
5. **The open box / gifting.** The assortment in its box, a hand selecting a single piece, ribbon and velvet, gold; the Valentine red / Christmas set. Sells the gesture, not just the product.
6. **Cocoa-dust still life / artisan provenance.** Truffles dredged in fine cocoa, dusty matte mounds used as plinths, raw cocoa pods cracked open beside scattered beans, the bean-to-bar story; warm light, brown monochrome, rustic surface. Craft and origin.
7. **Playful games & colour-block.** Bonbons as chess or checkers pieces, heart-chocolates in a tic-tac-toe grid, scrabble tiles, dominoes — staged into a play moment on a saturated seamless, overhead or low three-quarter, hands welcome. Modern, witty, very D2C.
8. **Hands & the editorial bite.** A hand presenting or snapping the bar, a model mid-bite (fashion-adjacent — fur, Vogue-cool, a bold lip), the bar held to the face. Carries brand personality and aspiration. Tower/stack and levitation (floating squares, a balanced bar) live here too — strong for youthful brands.
9. **Inclusion / ingredient pairing.** The chocolate with its flavour source — whole hazelnuts and almonds, a caramel pour, sea-salt flakes, freeze-dried raspberries, orange, cocoa nibs — fresh and just-placed, echoing the variant. Proves the flavour and the quality of what's inside.

### Chocolate substance & material rules (extends the engine's substance rules)

- **Temper is everything.** Well-tempered chocolate has a **mirror gloss and a crisp snap** — that sheen *is* the quality signal. Render it. **Bloom** (grey/white streaky fat or sugar haze) reads stale and is an instant reject — *unless* the brand is deliberately raw/rustic bean-to-bar, where a matte unconched surface is intentional.
- **The melt is thick and glossy.** Molten couverture is honey-to-syrup viscous — it flows slow, coats, ribbons, and pools with a soft sheen. Never a thin watery "syrup" stream. A controlled, indulgent melt sells craving; an accidental smeared/fingerprinted melt reads as damage — keep it deliberate.
- **The snap and the fracture.** Clean sharp break edges; the interior of a break is **matte** against the glossy exterior, and shows the crumb/grain of the chocolate (and any inclusions in cross-section).
- **Filled centres behave by type.** Caramel strings and pulls and runs slow; ganache is dense and holds a soft peak; liquid/gianduja flows; praline is grainy-smooth. Show the centre doing the right thing in any cut/bite shot.
- **Surface finishes.** Bonbon shells can be high-gloss enrobed, hand-painted with coloured cocoa-butter splatter/transfer, or matte velvet-sprayed; truffles are cocoa-dusted or coated. Cocoa powder is **fine and matte**, dredging and dusting, never a wet clump.
- **Moulded-bar geometry must be exact.** The bar's break-grid, the debossed brand mark or pattern in each square, proportions and bevels — reproduce them faithfully. Warped squares or invented debossing is an instant tell.
- **Inclusions read true:** whole nuts with their skins and sheen, sea-salt flakes catching light, glossy caramel, real fruit. Nothing plastic-looking.

### Chocolate light & surface

- **Luxury / gifting** → low-key warm directional light, deep controlled shadow, a single specular roll across the gloss; slate, polished stone, velvet, dark wood; bokeh; gold and burgundy accents.
- **Playful / D2C** → bright hard light, crisp sharp shadows, saturated seamless keyed to flavour/brand; clean and graphic.
- **Artisan / bean-to-bar** → soft warm window light, cocoa-brown monochrome, linen/wood/stone, visible cocoa dust in the air or on the surface.

### Chocolate default move

When the brief is thin for chocolate: **read the brand into one of the three worlds (luxury / playful / artisan) → lead with the temper gloss and the molten pour or the snap → if the piece is filled, add the oozing cross-section → set the light and surface to the chosen world (moody-dark / saturated colour-block / warm cocoa-brown) → add an inclusion-pairing or a hand for proof and personality.** Protect temper and geometry above all. Pull exact palette, surface, and mood from the Brand Profile first.

## Prepared & café beverages (coffee · matcha · tea · lattes)

A distinct mode within F&B. Use it when the product is a coffee, matcha, tea, or other prepared/café drink — including its packaged form (beans, grounds, a matcha pouch or tin, tea bags). The product is usually shown **twice**: as the **package** (the hero object) *and* as the **prepared drink** in a cup or glass.

**Core insight:** this sub-category sells a **ritual and a mood**, not just refreshment. It splits into two monochrome colour-worlds — frame the whole image in the drink's own colour:

- **Coffee** → warm, moody, browns / creams / amber; window light, deep shadows; wood, marble, stainless café surfaces; cozy, slow, grown-up.
- **Matcha** → fresh, vivid green monochrome; bright clean daylight; green / cream / tile surfaces; airy, wellness, "matcha-girl" lifestyle.

### Café archetypes

1. **The prep ritual — *the dominant café move.*** Whisking matcha (chasen in the bowl), the espresso pull from the portafilter, the milk pour with latte art forming, the moka-pot pour, cold milk meeting matcha/espresso over ice with the bloom/swirl. The *making* is the hero.
2. **Latte art / surface macro.** Top-down on the rosetta, the crema, the microfoam, the matcha foam — texture and barista craft.
3. **The pour / liquid bloom.** Espresso or matcha streaming into milk over ice, the brown/green clouding through, condensation on the iced glass.
4. **Drink-in-hand café lifestyle.** Holding the to-go cup on the street or at the café table; the outfit-plus-drink fashion-adjacent shot; multiple cups carried; a "cheers." The aspirational daily ritual — strong for brand social.
5. **Ingredient + tools still life.** Matcha powder + bamboo whisk + scoop + tin; coffee beans cascading from the bag; beans + pour-over + mug on stone or wood. This is where the **package (pouch / bag / tin) sits as hero among its tools** — it sells provenance and craft.
6. **Monochrome colour-world studio.** The entire frame keyed to the drink's colour (an all-green matcha set on green tile; a warm-brown coffee set). The aesthetic flex.
7. **Iced macro / refreshment.** Close on the iced latte — ice cubes, condensation, the layered gradient (milk bottom, espresso/matcha top). Cold-drink craving.

### Café substance rules

- **Coffee:** real glossy tan crema on espresso; microfoam with clean latte-art definition; a believable pour stream from pitcher or moka pot; brewed coffee's true depth and translucency over ice; a trace of steam off a hot cup; beans with an oil sheen.
- **Matcha:** true *vivid* green — never dull or army-green; fine clumping powder; a fine-bubble foam from whisking; iced layers (milk below, green above) with a real bloom where they meet; condensation on the glass.
- **Both:** the to-go cup, pouch, tin, or bag branding stays sharp and legible; latte art not warped; ice with real refraction.

### Café light & surface

- **Coffee** → warm directional window light, deep shadows, wood / marble / stainless, café ambiance.
- **Matcha** → bright fresh daylight, green / cream / tile, clean and minimal.

## Substance & material specifics (extends the engine's substance rules)

**Drinks**
- **Condensation & chill:** cold cans/bottles beaded with fresh water droplets; dunked in ice or an ice bath; frosted-cold surface. This is the #1 refreshment cue — use it.
- **The pour & fizz:** a real liquid stream into a glass, bubbles rising, carbonation, a small crown on landing; true liquid colour; ice with believable refraction.
- **Splash:** dynamic liquid around the can with real weight and droplets, never a flat sprite.
- **Sachets / powders (electrolyte, drink mix):** powder pouring into water, a dissolve plume, the sachet's foil reading true.
- **Glass bottles:** refraction and a controlled highlight; the cap/crown true.

**Food**
- **Crunch & texture:** real surface — the ridge of a chip, oil sheen, granola clusters, the matte of a puff, the gloss and snap of chocolate. Macro proves it.
- **The spill/cascade:** believable physics — chips and puffs scatter and stack the way they really fall; real crumbs; generous abundance.
- **Freshness garnish:** for granola/cereal, fresh berries and nuts scattered; nothing wilted or set too long.
- **Packaging:** foil reads metallic, matte stays matte, the bag's crinkle and tension look real; resealable zips and gussets correct.

**Both:** the brand's label, logo, flavour name, and callouts stay sharp and legible — warped flavour text is an instant reject.

## Default move (safe, on-brand F&B recipe)

When the brief is thin: **decide food or drink → key a saturated colour-block to the flavour/brand → hard sunny light with a crisp shadow →** lead with **packaging hero + contents proof** (a spill for food, a pour/condensation for drink) → add a **flavour-pairing** prop or a **hand** for energy → keep it craveable (food) or ice-cold (drink). For **coffee/matcha/tea**, switch to the Prepared & café beverages mode: pick the drink's colour-world, lead with the **prep ritual** or **ingredient-and-tools still life**, show the package + the prepared drink. Pull exact palette, surface, and mood from the Brand Profile first.

## Six-angle adaptation

The engine's six angles still apply; bias them for F&B:
- **Macro** is essential — it carries crunch (food) or cold/fizz (drink).
- **Three-quarter** flatters cans, bottles, and standing bags.
- **Overhead** is strong for flat-lay spreads, ranges, and lifestyle tables.
- Swap in a **spill/pour** frame and a **hands/lifestyle** frame over a plain side profile.
- Keep the colour world, light, and flavour story consistent so the set reads as one campaign.

## Category do-not list (on top of the Brand Profile's)

- No flat, even, shadowless light that kills energy and refreshment (unless it's a deliberately minimal premium brand).
- No drink without a chill/refreshment cue when refreshment is the promise — dry, room-temp-looking cans read stale.
- No fake fizz, flat sprite-splash, or sourceless bubbles.
- No spill that ignores physics (floating crumbs, impossible piles).
- No wilted, greasy-wrong, or stale-looking food — appetite is the job.
- No warped flavour names, callouts, or nutrition text.
- No neon colour-block forced onto a heritage/premium brand that wants warm and rustic — read the brand.
- **Chocolate:** no bloom (grey/white streaky fat or sugar haze) on a brand that promises gloss — reads stale; allowed only for deliberately raw/rustic bean-to-bar.
- **Chocolate:** no thin watery "syrup" pour, no accidental fingerprint/smear melt, no dull lifeless surface where temper is the promise.
- **Chocolate:** no warped bar-grid geometry or invented debossing in the squares; no plastic-looking bonbon shells.
